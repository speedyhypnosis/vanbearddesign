About Van Beard Jr.

As a graphic designer specializing in organizing information, I created these recipe collections to solve a common problem: finding soul food recipes that work together as complete dinner menus. While I don't cook, I recognized the need for well-coordinated recipe collections with consistent serving sizes and realistic timelines.

Using research tools and AI assistance, I've curated and organized traditional soul food recipes into cohesive dinner experiences. Each recipe book represents my skills as an information designer applied to conventional recipes, bringing organization and clarity to help you create memorable meals.

Every recipe collection includes AI-generated visual representations and standardized serving sizes for 6-8 people, with step-by-step timelines that coordinate the cooking process from start to finish.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why should I buy recipes from someone who doesn't cook?

My value isn't in creating new recipes but solving the meal planning problem. As an information designer, I've organized traditional recipes into coordinated timelines that eliminate kitchen chaos. You're purchasing professional organization skills for meal planning, not culinary expertise.

What makes these different from free online recipes?

The coordination system. Individual recipes are everywhere, but organizing 6 dishes to finish simultaneously requires planning. My collections provide standardized serving sizes, coordinated timelines, and a professional presentation that turns recipe hunting into seamless meal execution.

How quickly will I receive the download?

Immediately after purchase confirmation. You'll receive an email with secure download links that work for 7 days.

Can I buy just one volume first?

Yes, individual volumes are $15 each. The bundle saves you $5 if you want both, but you can start with whichever menu appeals most.

Are these recipes tested?

I've curated and organized these traditional soul food recipes using research tools and AI assistance. As a graphic designer rather than a cook, I focus on providing well-organized meal coordination systems. I recommend reviewing ingredients and techniques before cooking, and adjusting based on your kitchen and preferences.

What if my cooking times don't match the timeline?

Cooking times vary based on equipment and ingredients. Use the timeline as a guide, but rely on visual and temperature cues for doneness. Every kitchen is different, so some adjustments are normal.
